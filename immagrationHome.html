And this are reason people immigration

Economic Opportunities: Many people seek better job prospects, higher wages, and improved living standards in the US, where the economy offers more opportunities for financial advancement. Political Stability: Some individuals flee political instability, violence, or persecution in their home countries. The US is seen as a safer place where they can live with more freedom and security. Educational Opportunities: The US has many prestigious educational institutions and better educational resources, attracting students and professionals who seek advanced education and training. Family Reunification: People often move to join family members who are already living in the US. Family connections are a significant motivator for immigration. Healthcare: Access to better healthcare services can be a major factor, especially if the healthcare system in their home country is inadequate or inaccessible. Quality of Life: Many immigrants are drawn by the prospect of a higher quality of life, including better housing, infrastructure, and overall living conditions. South American communities often have strong cultural ties in the US, which can make the transition easier and offer a sense of belonging.
